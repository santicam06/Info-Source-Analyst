### 4. CLAIMS VERIFICATION

#### Main Claims of the Study
The study claimed to show that:
- A woman's bust size positively correlates with male drivers stopping to offer hitchhiking rides
- Increasing bra cup size increased success rate among male (but not female) drivers
- Based on 1,200 motorists tested

#### Verification Results

**Claims Cannot Be Verified - RETRACTED FOR IMPLAUSIBILITY**

The Perceptual and Motor Skills retraction notice (February 2025) explicitly states:

1. **"Low confidence that the study designs were implemented as described"**
   - Editors doubt the fieldwork actually occurred as reported
   - No mechanism for independent verification of field observations

2. **"Implausible or incorrectly analyzed data"**
   - Statistical analysis does not meet standards
   - Results are mathematically suspicious

3. **"Lack of ethics approval and informed consent"**
   - No evidence of institutional review board approval
   - No informed consent from motorists used in experiment

4. **"Antiquated reference lists"**
   - Cited works predate publication by average of 10-20 years
   - Unusually outdated for a 2007 publication

#### Additional Red Flags Identified by Researchers

Critics (Brown & Heathers, 2017) noted:
- **Excessive productivity:** How could one person conduct 10+ extensive field studies annually with proper rigor?
- **Implausible effect sizes:** The reported magnitudes of effects are unusually large
- **Impossible statistics:** Some reported numbers violate mathematical constraints given sample sizes
- **Fieldwork logistics:** The described experiments would require more time/resources than feasible for one researcher

## RESEARCH INTEGRITY CONTEXT

This case illustrates important principles about scientific integrity:

1. **Peer review is not infallible** - This article passed peer review in 2007 but showed red flags only became undeniable 15+ years later

2. **Reproducibility and plausibility matter** - When results are too good to be true or methodologically implausible, scrutiny is warranted

3. **Cumulative evidence model** - One questionable paper might pass through; a pattern across dozens of papers indicates systematic issues

4. **Platform credibility varies** - ResearchGate, Academia.edu, and similar platforms are NOT equivalent to journals; they have no quality gatekeeping
